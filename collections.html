Artifact Histories

Sunstone Amulet

This amulet is made from polished sunstone and dates back approximately 900 years. It was found in a ceremonial burial mound and may have been used for ritual purposes. Unique etchings suggest symbolic meanings associated with sunlight and fertility.

Ceremonial Whale Harpoon

This whale harpoon was carved from whale bone over 700 years ago. Found in a coastal village site alongside fishing tools, it likely served both functional and ceremonial roles, possibly symbolizing the community’s maritime identity and connection to whales.

Obsidian Writing Tablet

This tablet of obsidian is approximately 1200 years old, discovered in a domestic dwelling site. Its flat polished surface bears geometric carvings, likely used for teaching, record-keeping, or symbolic storytelling within its culture.

Sunstone Amulet — Collection Note

Made from polished sunstone, this amulet is roughly 900 years old. Found in a ceremonial burial mound, it likely held ritual significance. Unique etchings suggest symbolic connections to sunlight and fertility. Accompanied by small clay figurines, its discovery hints at ceremonial offerings and cultural symbolism in spiritual practices.

Ceremonial Whale Harpoon — Collection Note

Carved from whale bone around 700 years ago, this harpoon was found in a coastal village site alongside fishing tools. Its size and craftsmanship suggest ceremonial use, perhaps as a status symbol or in maritime rituals. Notably, the tip is engraved with geometric patterns indicating symbolic significance beyond hunting utility.

Obsidian Writing Tablet — Collection Note

This obsidian tablet, approximately 1200 years old, was discovered in a domestic dwelling. Its flat polished surface bears intricate geometric carvings, possibly for teaching, record-keeping, or storytelling. Found with small ceramic shards, the tablet provides insight into the intellectual and symbolic practices of its culture.
